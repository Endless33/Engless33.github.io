What is VRP?

VRP (Variable Routing Protocol) is a behavioral communication organism.

It treats routing, encryption and transport not as static configuration, but as a living system that reacts to observation, pressure and time.

Core ideas:
- Handshake: ephemeral X25519 keys, minimal exposure.
- Sealing: AEAD (ChaCha20-Poly1305) as the sealing layer.
- Behavior: pulse, rotation, mutation — the protocol "moves".
- Transport: adaptive, not married to a single tunnel.

VRP is not "a VPN provider". It is a protocol that can be embedded, extended and evolved. The public surface you see here is intentionally minimal and cinematic — the real organism lives in private repos, mutation logs, and the behavior of those who interact with it.

The goal is simple: make surveillance and static assumptions expensive, confusing and fragile — while keeping the protocol itself precise, documented and architected for long-term evolution.
